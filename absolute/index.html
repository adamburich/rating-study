Driving Behavior Rating Study — Single Clips

Thank you for participating. Please enter your name, then press Start.

Instructions

What you're doing: You'll watch short overhead traffic clips, one at a time. In each clip, one vehicle is outlined in cyan with a ▼ marker above it — that's the vehicle you're judging. The number on each vehicle is its speed in mph.

About speed: These clips are from a US highway. A high speed number isn't by itself the answer — judge how the driver behaves in relation to the traffic around them (following distance, lane changes, closing on other cars), not the number alone.

Your task for each clip, two answers:

1) Rate how dangerously the highlighted driver is behaving on a 0–100 scale, from 0 — no danger at all to 100 — extremely dangerous. Use the whole range as you see fit; there is no curve and no expected distribution.

2) Answer yes or no: "If you were monitoring this road for safety, would you flag this driver as dangerous?"

Also mark your confidence: low / medium / high.

Rules:

  • Work alone. Don't discuss with other raters.
  • Go with your gut; don't overthink.
  • You can replay each clip as many times as you like.
  • There are no trick questions and no "right" answer we're checking against — we want your judgment.
