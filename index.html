Instructions

What you're doing: You'll watch short overhead traffic clips. In each clip, one vehicle is outlined in cyan with a ▼ marker above it — that's the vehicle you're judging. The number on each vehicle is its speed in mph.

Your task: For each pair, you'll see two clips (A and B), each with one highlighted vehicle. Answer one question:

"Which highlighted driver is behaving more dangerously — creating more risk of a collision for themselves or others by how they're driving?"

Answer A, B, or one of two tie options: "Too close to call — both look risky" when both drivers concern you and you can't rank them, or "Neither looks dangerous" when nothing either driver does concerns you. Use the tie options sparingly — pick A or B whenever you can separate them at all. Also mark your confidence: low / medium / high.

Rules:

  • Work alone. Don't discuss with other raters.
  • Go with your gut; don't overthink.
  • You can replay each clip as many times as you like.
  • There are no trick questions and no "right" answer we're checking against — we want your judgment.
